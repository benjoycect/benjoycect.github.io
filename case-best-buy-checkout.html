Best Buy Checkout Redesign

Role: UX Director
Year: 2023
Team: 5 designers

Led the creation and execution of a research-backed roadmap to reduce customer friction and increase conversion of Best Buy's cart and checkout.

design strategy team leadership commerce product management

What We Were Solving

Checkout had long been a source of friction on BestBuy.com. Leadership asked my team to reimagine cart and checkout in a way that streamlined the experience while maintaining customer trust.

What Made This Hard

Checkout touches every customer, every product, and every fulfillment method. Small changes ripple across the experience and could easily introduce risk.

Core tension: Improve conversion without compromising reliability or overwhelming customers with change.

What I Did

I led design strategy, team execution, and executive alignment. We paired iterative design with evaluative research, reducing usability and value risk at each stage.

  • Facilitated research with real customers to reduce friction
  • Set vision and design principles for checkout redesign
  • Directed five designers across content and product design
  • Presented progress and outcomes to executive leadership

How We Worked

We used a product-model approach: discovery, exploration, evaluation, and iteration. Research showed that too many choices slowed customers down. We streamlined flows, simplified content, and used progressive disclosure to maintain confidence.

Design principle: Reduce choices, not confidence. Guide customers with progressive disclosure.

What Happened

The redesign lifted conversion, improved satisfaction, and reduced abandonment. It was recognized internally as a major success and validated the value of product-model UX work.

Strategic impact: Showed that customer-centered design could drive measurable business results.
